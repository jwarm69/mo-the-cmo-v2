# Bite Club Content Production Workflow & Optimization Process

## Daily Content Production Workflow

### Morning Routine (9:00-10:00 AM)

#### Performance Review (15 minutes)
```
Daily Checklist:
□ Review previous day's post performance
□ Check engagement rates vs benchmarks
□ Identify top-performing content types
□ Note any trending topics or viral content opportunities
□ Screenshot performance data for weekly review
```

#### Trend Research (15 minutes)
```
Platform Scanning:
□ TikTok Creative Center - trending sounds and hashtags
□ Instagram Reels - popular audio and effects
□ Campus hashtags (#UF, #UFStudents) for local trends
□ Competitor content analysis
□ News/events at UF that could tie into content
```

#### Content Planning Confirmation (15 minutes)
```
Daily Plan Review:
□ Confirm planned content aligns with calendar
□ Adjust for any trending opportunities
□ Verify equipment and location availability
□ Check restaurant partner availability if needed
□ Prepare props/materials for shoots
```

#### Community Management (15 minutes)
```
Engagement Tasks:
□ Respond to comments from last 24 hours
□ Reply to DMs and inquiries
□ Engage with UF student content using brand account
□ Monitor brand mentions and tagged content
□ Flag any negative feedback for response strategy
```

### Content Creation Block (10:00 AM - 2:00 PM)

#### Pre-Production Setup (30 minutes)
```
Equipment Preparation:
□ Charge phone/camera batteries
□ Set up tripod and lighting (ring light if indoor)
□ Test audio quality and external mic if needed
□ Prepare backdrop/location
□ Layout props, food items, or app screenshots

Script/Outline Preparation:
□ Review content template for the day
□ Adapt script to current trends/audio
□ Prepare text overlays and key messages
□ Time out content flow (hook, middle, CTA)
□ Have backup content ideas ready
```

#### Production Phase (2-3 hours)
```
Batch Creation Strategy:
□ Film multiple videos in similar setups
□ Create variations of successful content
□ Capture B-roll footage for future use
□ Take high-quality photos for feed posts
□ Record voiceovers for multiple videos

Content Types to Batch:
□ 3-4 TikTok videos (different templates)
□ 2-3 Instagram Reels
□ 1-2 Instagram feed posts
□ Story content for Instagram
□ YouTube Shorts adaptation
```

#### Quality Control Checklist
```
Technical Standards:
□ Video: Clear, well-lit, steady footage
□ Audio: Clear speech, balanced music levels
□ Framing: Vertical 9:16 format optimized
□ Duration: Platform-appropriate length
□ Text: Readable, well-timed overlays

Content Standards:
□ Brand voice consistency
□ Message clarity and authenticity
□ Strong hook within first 3 seconds
□ Clear call-to-action
□ Appropriate hashtag usage
```

### Post-Production & Scheduling (2:00-4:00 PM)

#### Editing Workflow (90 minutes)
```
Editing Pipeline:
1. Import and organize footage
2. Rough cut assembly (follow template structure)
3. Add text overlays and graphics
4. Audio mixing and music integration
5. Color correction and brightness optimization
6. Export in platform-specific specifications

Editing Apps Recommendation:
□ CapCut (free, feature-rich, trending effects)
□ InShot (simple, good for beginners)
□ Adobe Premiere Rush (advanced features)
□ Native platform editors for quick edits
```

#### Content Scheduling (30 minutes)
```
Scheduling Tools:
□ Later.com (Instagram/TikTok scheduling)
□ Hootsuite (multi-platform management)
□ Buffer (content calendar management)
□ Native platform scheduling (Instagram Creator Studio)

Optimal Posting Times:
□ TikTok: 12:00 PM, 7:00 PM, 9:00 PM EST
□ Instagram Reels: 11:00 AM, 2:00 PM, 5:00 PM EST
□ Instagram Stories: 8:00 AM, 1:00 PM, 7:00 PM EST
□ YouTube Shorts: 3:00 PM, 8:00 PM EST
```

### Evening Optimization (4:00-6:00 PM)

#### Performance Monitoring (30 minutes)
```
Real-Time Analytics:
□ Monitor first-hour performance of new posts
□ Check engagement velocity vs previous content
□ Respond to early comments to boost engagement
□ Share to Instagram Stories if performing well
□ Cross-promote on other platforms if viral potential
```

#### Community Engagement (60 minutes)
```
Active Engagement Strategy:
□ Reply to all comments within first 2 hours
□ Engage with similar content in niche
□ Comment on UF student posts to build community
□ Share user-generated content to Stories
□ Monitor competitor activity and engagement
```

## Content Optimization Frameworks

### A/B Testing Schedule

#### Weekly Testing Calendar
```
Monday: Hook Variations
□ Test different opening lines/visuals
□ Same content, different first 3 seconds
□ Measure completion rates and engagement

Wednesday: Format Variations  
□ Test video vs carousel vs static posts
□ Different aspect ratios or visual styles
□ Measure click-through and conversion rates

Friday: CTA Variations
□ Test different call-to-action placement/wording
□ "Download now" vs "Try Bite Club" vs "Link in bio"
□ Measure app store visits and downloads
```

#### Testing Documentation Template
```
Test Setup:
- Variable being tested: [Hook/Format/CTA/etc.]
- Variants: A vs B (describe differences)
- Test duration: [X days]
- Success metric: [Engagement rate/CTR/Conversions]
- Audience: [Same targeting parameters]

Results:
- Variant A performance: [Specific metrics]
- Variant B performance: [Specific metrics]  
- Statistical significance: [Yes/No]
- Winner: [A/B/Inconclusive]
- Key learnings: [What worked and why]
- Next actions: [Scale winner/Test further/Abandon]
```

### Content Refresh Strategy

#### Content Lifecycle Management
```
Content Performance Stages:
1. Launch (0-7 days): Monitor initial reception
2. Peak (1-4 weeks): Identify viral potential, amplify
3. Plateau (1-3 months): Analyze for replication
4. Decline (3+ months): Refresh or retire format

Refresh Triggers:
□ Engagement rate drops 25% below average
□ Content format hasn't performed in 2 weeks
□ Platform algorithm changes affect reach
□ Competitor copies format successfully
□ Audience feedback indicates content fatigue
```

#### Template Evolution Process
```
Monthly Template Review:
1. Identify templates with declining performance
2. Analyze successful elements to preserve
3. Research new trending formats to incorporate
4. Test hybrid approaches combining old + new
5. Gradually phase out underperforming templates
6. Document new template performance baselines
```

## Quality Assurance & Brand Safety

### Content Approval Workflow

#### Self-Review Checklist
```
Before Posting Checklist:
□ Content aligns with brand voice and values
□ Claims are accurate and substantiated
□ No sensitive/controversial topics addressed
□ Text overlays are spell-checked and readable
□ Audio levels are balanced and clear
□ Hashtags are relevant and appropriate
□ Call-to-action is clear and compliant
□ No personal information accidentally included
```

#### Escalation Protocol
```
When to Seek Approval:
□ Content involving specific savings claims
□ Partnerships or collaborations with others
□ Response to negative feedback or criticism
□ New content formats not previously tested
□ Content that could be considered controversial
□ Crisis response or damage control content
```

### Brand Monitoring & Response

#### Daily Brand Monitoring
```
Monitoring Tasks:
□ Check tagged content and mentions
□ Review comments for sentiment and issues
□ Monitor competitor mention and positioning
□ Track hashtag usage and user-generated content
□ Identify potential brand safety issues
```

#### Response Strategy Framework
```
Positive Feedback:
□ Like, reply, and thank users
□ Share to Stories (with permission)
□ Consider featuring in future content
□ Engage authentically to build community

Neutral Questions:
□ Respond helpfully and informatively
□ Direct to appropriate resources
□ Use as content inspiration for FAQs
□ Maintain friendly, student-focused tone

Negative Feedback:
□ Respond quickly and empathetically
□ Take conversations private when appropriate
□ Address legitimate concerns transparently
□ Learn from feedback to improve service
□ Document patterns for product team
```

This comprehensive workflow ensures consistent, high-quality content production while maintaining optimization and brand safety standards throughout the growth marketing campaign.
